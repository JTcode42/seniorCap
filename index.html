Blockchain Basics

A special database relying on cryptography to ensure data integrity

Traditional data networks lack diversification and security. All of the information is stored together by a centralized entity such as a company or government. As a result, one must put faith in these centralized agencies to secure their network and its respective information.

The above practice is what we are all acustomed to and it works well until the centralized entity tampers with the data (i.e. embezzlement, kleptocracy), begins misusing information, is compromised by rogue employees, or is most commonly hacked due to a single point of failure.

A Possible Solution:

A database that has multiple copies all of which automatically update in unison. If a copy gets compromised the other copies could highlight the problem and resecure the database.

  • Immutable history
